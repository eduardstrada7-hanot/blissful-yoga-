When people hear "yoga therapy," most picture a very calm person doing very advanced poses in a very white room.

That's not it.

Yoga therapy is a clinical application of yoga — meaning it uses the full toolkit of yoga (breath, movement, meditation, lifestyle practices, nervous system regulation) to address specific health conditions and support wellbeing. It's one-on-one. It's tailored entirely to you. And it is nothing like a yoga class.

## What it actually looks like

A yoga therapy session with me might include:

- Gentle movement adapted to exactly your body today
- Breathwork designed to shift your nervous system state
- Guided body-scan meditation
- Discussion about what's showing up in your life and how that's living in your body
- Simple practices to take home

It might not include anything that looks like "yoga" at all. Some sessions are almost entirely breath and stillness.

## Who is it for?

Yoga therapy is recognized by IAYT (the International Association of Yoga Therapists) as a complementary health approach. The research supports its use for:

- Anxiety and depression (alongside professional mental health care)
- Chronic pain and fatigue
- Stress and burnout
- Sleep disorders
- Trauma recovery
- Grief and major life transitions

If you're dealing with any of these — or something else entirely — the best thing to do is book a Discovery Call and tell me what's going on. I'll give you an honest answer about whether yoga therapy is a fit.

## What it's not

Yoga therapy is not a replacement for medical care or psychotherapy. It's a complement — something that works *alongside* whatever care you're already receiving. I always recommend that clients continue working with their doctors, therapists, and other providers.

And it's absolutely not only for people who can do yoga. Most of my yoga therapy clients have never taken a yoga class in their lives, and some have mobility limitations that make traditional yoga inaccessible. That's kind of the point.

If you have a body and a nervous system, yoga therapy is for you.
