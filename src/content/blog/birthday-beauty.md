I have a complicated relationship with birthdays.

Not because I mind getting older — I genuinely don't. Each year has given me something: more patience, more clarity, more willingness to say *I don't know* and mean it as an open door instead of a confession.

What I mind is the pressure to look like I'm not getting older.

I teach a practice that asks us to arrive as we are. And yet, for years, I would scan photos of myself and immediately look for what to fix. The lines by my eyes. The softness in my face. The way my body has changed with the years.

There is a particular cruelty in aging as a woman in a visual world. Every ad, every product, every "anti-aging" promise is a tiny vote for the idea that what you are now is not enough. That the work of staying beautiful is the work of staying as young as possible for as long as possible.

I don't believe that anymore.

Beauty, in the way yoga has taught me to understand it, is not about appearance. It's about presence. It's about being so fully in your body, so honest in your face, so willing to be seen — that other people feel a little more permission to be themselves in your presence.

That's what I want to offer. Not youth. Not performance. Just presence.

On this birthday, I am choosing to call that beautiful.

*With love,*
*Veronica*
